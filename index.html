9.Define BigData,SmallData and MetaData?

Big Data: Big Data refers to large and diverse datasets that grow in volume and complexity over time. It can be structured, unstructured, or semi-structured. Big data often comes from data mining and arrives in multiple formats. Its used in machine learning, predictive modeling, and other advanced analytics to solve business problems and make informed decisions.

10.Explain Legacy Data?

Legacy data refers to information that is stored in outdated or obsolete systems, formats, or technologies. This data was created years ago and may not be actively used by an organization anymore. However, it is often crucial for legal, regulatory, or historical reasons. Examples of legacy data include customer records, financial data, emails, documents, databases, spreadsheets, presentations, and data stored on removable storage media which is no longer widely used.

11.What is DataQuality and DataSecurity?

Data Quality: Data quality refers to the overall utility of a dataset and its ability to be easily processed and analyzed for other uses. It is an integral part of data governance that ensures that your organizations data is fit for purpose. Data quality dimensions include completeness, conformity, consistency, accuracy, and integrity.
Data Security: Data security is the process of safeguarding digital information throughout its entire life cycle to protect it from corruption, theft, or unauthorized access. It covers everything—hardware, software, storage devices, and user devices; access and administrative controls; and organizations policies and procedures.

12.What is datasteward?

A data steward is a role within an organization that oversees the quality and fitness for purpose of the organizations data assets, including the metadata for those data assets.
Data stewards have a specialist role that utilizes an organizations data governance processes, policies, guidelines, and responsibilities for administering the organizations entire data in compliance with policy and/or regulatory obligations.

2.List and explain Applications of AI in healthcare?

Disease Diagnosis: AI can help in diagnosing diseases by analyzing medical images, patient records, and other data. It can identify patterns and anomalies that might be missed by human eyes.

Improving Patient Experience: AI can be used to improve the patient experience by providing personalized care and reducing wait times.
Healthcare Administration: AI can help streamline administrative tasks in healthcare, such as scheduling appointments, managing patient records, and billing. It can also help in identifying and preventing healthcare fraud.
Public Health: AI can be used to analyze public health data to identify trends and predict outbreaks.

3.What is BigData and 3V's of data?

Big Data refers to extremely large and diverse collections of structured, unstructured, and semi-structured data that continues to grow exponentially over time. These datasets are so huge and complex in volume, velocity, and variety, that traditional data management systems cannot store, process, and analyze them. Big Data is used in machine learning, predictive modeling, and other advanced analytics to solve business problems and make informed decisions.
Volume: This refers to the amount of data. Big data deals with data in large volumes that can reach unprecedented heights. Its estimated that 2.5 quintillion bytes of data is created each day.
Velocity: This measures how fast the data is coming in. Some data will come in real-time, whereas others will come in fits and starts, sent to us in batches.
Variety: This refers to the different types of data. Data was once collected from one place and delivered in one format. Now, it is being presented in non-traditional forms, like video, text, pdf, and graphics on social media, as well as via tech such as wearable devices.

4.What is Fragmented Data and explain hurdles with fragmented data.

Fragmented Data refers to the condition where data is scattered across various different systems and locations, such as clouds or on-premises. This fragmentation can prevent organizations from achieving full visibility and control of the data, and from maximizing the potential value of the data.
When data is fragmented, it can lead to several challenges:
Inefficient use of computer capacity: When data is distributed across different silos, it can cause computer capacity to be used inefficiently.
Difficulty in maintaining regulatory compliance: Without complete visibility into what data is being tracked, where its being stored, and who has access to it, organizations can face serious risks of being non-compliant with privacy laws and regulations.
Decreased data quality: Data fragmentation can impact the accuracy, completeness, timeliness, accessibility, and consistency of the data.
Reduced operational efficiency: The need to manage and coordinate multiple proprietary systems and user interfaces, each requiring specialist understanding, can compromise operational efficiency.
Increased storage costs: Keeping large volumes of data that are poorly or not exploited is quite costly.
Increased exposure to data breaches:
Fragmented data can sometimes escape data governance and security strategies, consequences that also increase exposure to data breaches. Data fragmentation is often accidental as organizations store more and more information to benefit the business. However, when teams no longer have complete visibility into their data, fragmentation becomes a considerable challenge. For these reasons, organizations are working to eliminate mass data fragmentation.

5.Explain in detail "Big Data Tool"?

Big Data tools are software utilities designed to analyze, process, and extract information from extremely large datasets that traditional data processing tools cannot handle. These tools help in managing, storing, retrieving, and processing Big Data. They are used in various sectors like healthcare, finance, retail, and more to make informed decisions, predict trends, and improve operational efficiency.
Here are some popular Big Data tools:
Apache Hadoop: Its a Java-based open-source platform used to store and process big data. It is built on a cluster system that allows the system to process data efficiently and let the data run parallel. It can process both structured and unstructured data from one server to multiple computers.
Apache Cassandra: Its an open-source NoSQL distributed database used to fetch large amounts of data. Its praised by many tech companies due to its high scalability and availability without compromising speed and performance.
Microsoft Azure: Its a cloud computing service created by Microsoft for building, testing, deploying, and managing applications and services through Microsoft-managed data centers.
Cloudera: Its a software platform that provides an enterprise-level data management solution. Its a unified platform that allows data engineering, data warehousing, machine learning, and analytics.
Tableau: Its a data visualization tool that helps in simplifying raw data into an easily understandable format.
